Overview about the faculty

The Faculty of Computer and Information Systems at Minia University is a hub of innovation and digital transformation. Through modern curricula, research opportunities, and partnerships with the tech industry, the faculty equips students to lead and innovate in the ever-evolving world of technology.

General Program

(Years 1 & 2)

All students start with a common foundation in IS and computing.

Core Topics Covered:

Fundamentals of Programming

Learn basic coding skills and logic building using modern languages.

Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)

Understand classes, objects, inheritance, and modular code design.

Web Fundamentals

Explore HTML, CSS, and basic JavaScript to build simple websites and understand how the web works.

Database Concepts and Design

Explore how to store, manage, and retrieve data efficiently.

Introduction to Information Systems

Discover how systems are structured to solve real-world problems.

Data Structures and Algorithms

Study how data is organized and manipulated for optimal performance.

Basic Mathematics for Computing

Strengthen your logic with essential math topics like logic, sets, and functions.

Goal

By the end of the second year, students will have a strong theoretical and practical foundation, helping them choose the right department for specialization.

Computer Science (CS) Program

(Years 3 & 4)

Students dive deeper into advanced computing concepts and practical skills.

Year 3

Image Processing

Learn the basics of manipulating and analyzing digital images.

Artificial Intelligence

Understand AI fundamentals, including logic, learning models, and neural networks.

Operating Systems

Explore OS concepts like memory management, process scheduling, and file systems.

Computer Architecture & Engineering

Study how computers are structured and operate at the hardware level.

Database Systems

Advanced handling, design, and optimization of databases.

Year 4

Intelligent Systems

Develop smart systems using AI principles, often with real-world applications.

Selected Topics in Computer Science

Explore recent trends, technologies, and research areas.

Data Storage and Retrieval

Study large-scale storage solutions and efficient data access techniques.

Job Opportunities in Computer Science

  • Software Engineer
  • AI Specialist
  • Data Scientist
  • Systems Architect
  • Cybersecurity Analyst
  • Researcher in Tech Companies or Institutions
  • Startup Developer
  • Global Enterprise IT Specialist

Goal:

Equip students with the technical depth and project experience to pursue careers or research in fields such as AI, software engineering, systems design, and more.

Information Systems (IS) program

Focused on business-oriented systems, databases, data management, and decision support.

Year 3

Geographic Information Systems (GIS)

Learn how spatial data supports real-world applications in planning and decision-making.

Operating Systems

Understand process scheduling, memory management, and system calls.

Decision Support Systems

Design systems that assist in making strategic, data-driven decisions.

Computer Graphics

Explore rendering techniques, animation, and interface graphics.

Software Engineering

Study the life cycle of software from planning and analysis to design and maintenance.

Software Development

Apply practical software development techniques in a capstone project.

Database Fundamentals

Strengthen your foundation in database systems, SQL, and schema design.

Spreadsheet Engineering

Use advanced spreadsheet tools and functions for modeling and analysis.

Year 4

Distributed Databases

Learn distributed architecture, data replication and consistency models.

Advanced Database Design

Focus on performance tuning, ER modeling, and normalization strategies.

Information Systems Quality Assurance

Evaluate and improve system quality through testing, validation, and documentation.

Information Security and Assurance

Study cybersecurity principles including encryption, authentication, and system hardening.

E-Commerce

Build systems for digital marketplaces and online transactions.

Data Storage and Retrieval

Handle efficient storage, indexing, and large-scale data access.

Data Communication

Understand how information flows across networks through various protocols.

Database Administration

Manage and secure databases, perform backup and recovery, and ensure availability.

Special Topics in Information Systems (1 & 2)

Explore emerging trends and research areas in the IS field.

Information Systems Quality Assurance

Learn system quality metrics, testing frameworks, and auditing tools.

  • Database Administrator

  • Systems Analyst

  • IT roles in Business, Government, Healthcare, and Technology

Goal:
